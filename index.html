Welcome to the High-Performance Human Interfacing Laboratory!
We're a new lab starting Fall 2025, led by P.I. Cameron Taylor.

In the Hi-PHI Lab, we develop transformative
human interfacing technologies
that enable a new era of human health.

At MIT, in collaboration with Brown University,
Dr. Taylor developed magnetomicrometry,
the first technology to be used for real-time direct
muscle tissue length tracking in humans.

Like the work we do here, magnetomicrometry spanned many fields,
including human physiology, electromagnetics, medical device design,
electronics, and algorithm design.

For instance, the first magnetomicrometry proof of concept
was a benchtop test of the
world's fastest magnet tracking algorithm.

The world is flooded with wearable technologies,
but none of those sensors can precisely, robustly tell you
what is happening inside your nerves and muscles.

In the Hi-PHI Lab, we're building those missing sensors.
We design devices that can sense, stimulate, and image
the human body using novel electromagnetic interfaces.
Our vision is a world where accessible, minimally-invasive
technologies transform lives by restoring ability
to persons with movement disorders
